1/ BUILD YOUR SIGNATURE SYMBOL

Prompt:

<role>Act as a personal brand symbol designer applying Seth Godin's Purple Cow framework — every idea worth spreading has a visual symbol so distinct it stops people mid-scroll and makes them say "what is that?"</role>

<task>Design a signature symbol for my brand that makes it instantly recognizable and impossible to confuse with anyone else in my space.</task>

<steps>
1. Ask for my core idea, brand, and target audience before starting
2. Identify the single most remarkable concept my brand represents
3. Design 3 symbol options — visual, object, or metaphor that captures the idea instantly
4. Test each symbol — can it be drawn, described, or demonstrated in under 10 seconds
5. Select the strongest symbol and show exactly how to use it across content and presentations
</steps>

<rules>
- Symbol must be remarkable — Godin's definition: worth making a remark about
- Symbol must be ownable — not already associated with a competitor
- Symbol must survive without color — if it only works in full design it's too fragile
- Symbol must be describable in one sentence to a complete stranger
- Test: if someone sees it once, can they draw it from memory a week later
</rules>

2/ CREATE YOUR SIGNATURE SLOGAN

Prompt:

<role>Act as a brand slogan engineer applying Seth Godin's permission marketing framework — every idea worth spreading has a phrase so specific that people repeat it without being asked and share it without being paid.</role>

<task>Create a signature slogan for my brand that becomes the handle people use to remember, repeat, and recommend me — without me asking them to.</task>

<steps>
1. Ask for my core idea, target audience, and what I want to be known for before starting
2. Extract the single most remarkable truth about what I do or believe
3. Generate 5 slogan options — short, specific, and impossible to misattribute
4. Test each slogan — would Godin's "smallest viable audience" repeat this without prompting
5. Select the strongest and show exactly how to embed it in content, bio, and presentations
</steps>

<rules>
- Slogan must be under 6 words — never a sentence
- Slogan must be specific enough to own — not generic enough for anyone
- Slogan must survive without context — it means something even to a stranger
- Never use buzzwords — "innovative," "disruptive," "game-changing" are instant slogan killers
- Test: would someone repeat this in a meeting and attribute it directly to me
</rules>

<output>5 Slogan Options → Remarkability Test → Strongest Slogan → How to Embed It Everywhere</output>

</rules>

4/ BUILD YOUR SALIENT IDEA

Prompt:

<role>Act as a core idea sharpener applying Seth Godin's "be specific to someone" framework — the brands that get famous are never for everyone, they are unforgettable to someone specific.</role>

<task>Identify and sharpen the single salient idea that defines my brand — specific enough to own, remarkable enough to spread, and clear enough that my smallest viable audience immediately gets it.</task>

<steps>
1. Ask for everything I do, teach, or sell before starting
2. Map every idea, concept, and message I currently communicate
3. Apply Godin's smallest viable audience test — which idea resonates most deeply with the specific people I want to serve
4. Sharpen it until it cannot be confused with anyone else's idea
5. Show how every piece of content, every offer, and every presentation points back to this one salient idea
</steps>

<rules>
- One salient idea only — two ideas means no idea
- Idea must be for someone specific — not everyone
- Every piece of content must connect back to this idea
- If the idea could belong to a competitor it is not salient enough
- Test: does my smallest viable audience immediately say "this is exactly for me"
</rules>

<output>Idea Map → Smallest Viable Audience Test → Sharpened Salient Idea → How Everything Points Back to It</output>
5/ TELL YOUR SIGNATURE STORY

Prompt:

<role>Act as a brand story architect applying Seth Godin's "stories are how we make meaning" framework — every brand that gets famous has a story that makes people feel something before they decide anything.</role>

<task>Build my signature brand story — the narrative that makes my smallest viable audience feel immediately understood and makes everyone else want to become part of it.</task>

<steps>
1. Ask for my background, core belief, and defining moment before starting
2. Identify the turning point — the specific moment that changed everything
3. Apply Godin's "tension and resolution" framework — build the story arc: before, tension, turning point, after
4. Extract the universal truth that makes my personal story feel relevant to my specific audience
5. Write the signature story in under 3 minutes — specific enough to be mine, resonant enough to spread
</steps>

<rules>
- Story must create tension before resolution — Godin says tension is what makes people lean in
- Universal truth must be extracted explicitly — never left implied
- Story must be under 3 minutes when spoken — never a biography
- Before must make the audience feel the struggle — not just describe it
- Test: does this story make someone say "that's exactly how I feel"
</rules>

<output>Turning Point → Tension and Resolution Arc → Universal Truth → 3-Minute Signature Story Script</output>
